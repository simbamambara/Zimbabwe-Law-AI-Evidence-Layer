# Data Governance Policy

## Purpose

This policy governs evidence data used by Zimbabwe Law AI from collection through retirement.

## Principles

1. Purpose limitation: process data only for documented legal-information, research, evaluation, and approved AI purposes.
2. Provenance: preserve source identifiers, occurrence records, hashes, transformation history, and review decisions.
3. Data minimisation: expose only the fields required for the approved use.
4. Human accountability: legal, editorial, privacy, security, and release decisions remain assigned to authorised persons.
5. Controlled access: full-text and regulatory evidence remain private unless a release is explicitly approved.
6. Reproducibility: material transformations must be scripted, versioned, tested, and logged.
7. Correction: inaccurate, restricted, or disputed records must enter the correction, suppression, or takedown workflow.
8. Retention: source, curated, public, audit, and operational records must have documented retention decisions.

## Roles requiring assignment

- Accountable executive or data controller
- Data-protection lead
- Information-security lead
- Legal and editorial lead
- Technical system owner
- Records-management owner

## Release control

No dataset or derived AI capability may be released merely because technical validation passes. Release also requires documented authority, privacy review, security review, legal/editorial approval, and a defined correction route.
